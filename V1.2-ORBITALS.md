# Tidal Orbit — v1.2 design: Orbitals VI–X (second wave)

Status: design/brainstorm. No code yet. v1.1 is in App Review.

Wave two theme: **exotic spacetime** — wormholes, pulsars, cosmic strings,
collapsing star. Mirrors wave one's escalation shape:
2D intro → 3D intro → 2D harder → 3D harder → survival/boss finale.

One-button discipline: every mechanic must resolve to "flip or don't." No new
inputs. Thresholds extend to 500/600/700/800/900; "Start From" and the
per-orbital speed ramp generalize for free.

---

## The five slots

**VI — "Wormholes" (2D, gentle intro).** Base 2D pendulum field; twin linked
portals blink in sporadically; enter one, exit its partner. Taught as the
SHORTCUT mode (see below) so players meet the mechanic when it helps them.

**VII — "Pulsar" (3D, new tunnel mechanic).** Options to pick from:
- Rhythmic pulse: gaps periodically pinch nearly shut then reopen — a timing
  read layered on the flip.
- Rotating tunnel: the whole barrier field corkscrews around the axis, so the
  flip must account for rotation.

**VIII — "Cosmic Strings" (2D, harder).** Sweeping deadly laser lines rotate
across the field (clock-hand style); flip to stay on the safe side as they
pass. Wormholes return in CHAOS mode. O3's harder cousin.

**IX — "Wormhole Tunnel" (3D, harder).** The 3D tunnel + wormholes (paired
rings you fly through to jump forward/sideways) + the O4 vertical drift. The
wave's hardest weaving challenge; combines everything the wave taught.

**X — "Supernova" (finale, survival/boss).** Reuse the O5 top-down arena
engine. Central star periodically goes supernova, firing expanding shockwave
rings — each ring has one gap you must line up with to survive. Wormholes to
reposition across the arena. Alt finale flavor: "three-body" (three gravity
wells yanking the orb around in chaotic orbits) for pure gravity chaos instead
of shockwaves.

Reuse: VII & IX build on `three3d.js`; X builds on the O5 arena; VI & VIII are
2D-field variants like O3. New mechanics on proven engines.

## Wormhole mechanic — detailed design (the anchor)

The hook: it inverts the core instinct. Normally you AVOID hazards; a wormhole
is one you sometimes want to AIM for.

### Orbital VI = Option A: escape hatch. LOCKED decisions
- **Flavor: escape hatch.** Portals are a safe, optional TOOL — the hazard
  stays the walls. Fantasy: "the gap is on the far side and my swing can't
  carry me there in time — a portal drops me onto the gap side with a beat to
  spare."
- **2D geometry note.** In the 2D pendulum the orb is fixed at `ORB_Y` and only
  moves horizontally (flip gravity); walls scroll down. So a wormhole
  REPOSITIONS HORIZONTALLY — it can't skip you vertically.
- **Both portals safe.** Touching one never kills; it teleports.
- **Enter by intent.** Steer into the near portal by flipping toward it. No
  auto-grab — that's the skill.
- **Exit is gentle.** Pop out at the twin's x, horizontal speed reset to ~0,
  still at `ORB_Y`. Predictable, no disorientation.
- **Free repositioning tool** (NOT hand-authored pairs). Portals spawn
  procedurally as a repositioning tool the player learns to exploit
  opportunistically — not placed as the guaranteed solution to a specific wall.
- **Optional insurance** (NOT mandatory). Walls stay solvable the normal way by
  swinging; the portal is optional insurance, never the only path. (Mandatory
  portals are saved for the harder VIII/IX.)
- **Telegraph.** Each portal fades in with an expanding warning ring ~0.4s
  before active, plus a soft hum. Never a surprise / cheap death.
- **Re-entry lock.** Exit ring inert ~0.5s so the orb doesn't fall back
  through and loop.
- **Reward the read.** Drop a couple coins just past the exit so taking the
  jump feels rewarded.
- **One pair at a time** early; color-matched rings (e.g. cyan pair) so it's
  obvious where you come out.

### Escalation across the wave (later orbitals)
- **Risk/reward** (mid): coins clustered on the far side; taking the portal is
  optional greed.
- **Chaos / mandatory** (VIII/IX): portals reposition unpredictably and/or some
  walls are only solvable via the portal; re-read the field fast under pressure.
